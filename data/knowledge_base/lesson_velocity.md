# Physics — Lesson 5: Velocity

**Subject:** Physics (A/L)
**Topic:** Kinematics — Velocity and speed

## Definition

**Velocity** is the rate of change of displacement with respect to time. It is a vector quantity, which means it has both magnitude and direction.

Speed is the magnitude of velocity only (scalar). A car going round a track at constant speed still has changing velocity because direction changes.

## Formula

```
v = Δs / Δt
```

Where:
- `v` = velocity (m/s)
- `Δs` = change in displacement (m)
- `Δt` = change in time (s)

Average velocity = total displacement / total time (not total distance).

## Example

A student walks 40 m east in 10 s, then 30 m west in 10 s.

- Total displacement = 40 − 30 = 10 m east
- Total time = 20 s
- Average velocity = 10 / 20 = **0.5 m/s east**

Total distance = 70 m, but average **speed** = 70/20 = 3.5 m/s — different from average velocity.

## Common mistakes

1. Confusing speed with velocity
2. Using distance instead of displacement in the formula
3. Forgetting direction when stating velocity

## Exam tip

Always state units (m/s) and direction when the question asks for velocity.
